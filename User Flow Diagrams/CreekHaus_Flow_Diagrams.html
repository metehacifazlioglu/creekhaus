How the data flows

This is the point of the whole system. A worker logs once; the record travels up on its own. Nothing is entered twice, and no one on the floor has to know which sheet or report it lands in.
Worker captures
Daily Update or Incident Report — voice, text, or photo. Tap, speak, done. Voice is transcribed server-side; audio + transcript both stored.
Manager reviews
Submissions land in the manager's Reports queue by category and status. The lead acknowledges, follows up, and owns the open/close log for the shift.
Executive sees patterns
The web dashboard aggregates over days — recurring issues, categories, history — turning one-off notes into operating memory and reward/concern signals.
Two things make this real, not theoretical:  (1) Logging is anchored to the named shift lead (FOH / BOH / premise) at open, close, and transition — not left to whoever's on the floor.  (2) Because the data is intentionally lean, the dashboards should read as "did the responsible person log," not "silence = all good."
